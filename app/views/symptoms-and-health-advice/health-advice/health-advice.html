Health advice

! Important:

It is your legal duty to stay at home and self-isolate. This is to keep you and others safe.

If you are the parent or guardian of a child under 18 who tested positive for COVID-19, you are responsible for making sure they self-isolate.

The last day of your 10-day self-isolation is Wednesday 2 February (including this date).

You can begin taking lateral flow tests from day 5 in your self-isolation period. This is to check if you can end self-isolation early.

Leaving self-isolation early

If you have a negative lateral flow test result for 2 days in a row, and do not have a high temperature, you can stop testing and leave self-isolation early. Do not begin testing before day 5 of your self-isolation period.

While you test positive, or if you have a high temperature, continue to self-isolate until Wednesday 2 February (including this date). You do not need to continue testing after this date.

Do:

  • tell your employer about your isolation period (this is a legal requirement), and get an isolation note if they request one
  • read the essential advice and guidance about how to self-isolate
  • find out what practical, financial and mental health support is available to help while you self-isolate
  • if you took a rapid lateral flow test, take a PCR test within 2 days to confirm the result

We will give you links to advice and support after you've completed your Test and Trace information.

Don't:

  • do not go to your place of work or education
  • do not go to a GP surgery or hospital (in a medical emergency, phone 999)
  • do not go to indoor or outdoor public areas, like shops or parks
  • do not use public transport or taxis
