HPEP Privacy Notice

Published [DD] [Month] 2022

Summary of Initiative/Policy

Currently, the UK Health Security Agency ("UKHSA") are unable to rapidly set-up and execute new or amended health protection campaigns, especially those with large digital elements.

We are therefore developing the Health Protection Engagement Platform (HPEP) which will deliver a technology capability to enable us to create and manage health protection campaigns quickly.

Data Controller

The Data Controller is UKHSA who are registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (Reg Number Z5571792)

What personal data we collect

We may need to collect the following pieces of personal information; Your name, address, postcode, date of birth, age, mobile or home telephone number, email address and contact preferences.

We may need to collect the following pieces of health data; Information regarding your physical or mental health condition.

We need to collect this data to be able to identify an individual that might be at risk of a health threat (e.g. Avian Flu). We will use this data to contact the identified individual, to be able to obtain their symptom status to be able to monitor, educate and contain an outbreak.

How we use your data (purposes)

We require your personal details to be able to send you information and ask questions to better understand your health status or symptom status for an identified health threat. We will also use this information to personalise your communications where desirable for example using your name to address you in a message. We will also use this data to understand how you would prefer to receive information; home, phone or email.

Legal basis for processing personal data

Under the Article 6 of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the lawful bases we rely on for processing this information are:

  • (e) Necessary task in the public interest or controller's official authority

In addition, under Article 9 of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we are required to identify an additional lawful basis to process health data. These are:

  • (i) Public Health

Data Processors and other recipients of personal data

For the first release of the Health Protection Engagement Platform your data will be contained within the UKHSA and Salesforce systems and sub processors (e.g., SMS providers), in a secure way.

Salesforce is a technology solution to enable UKHSA to create and manage health protection campaigns, helping to identify and enrol citizens relating to a specific health threat and communicate effectively with each citizen using SMS messages, emails and other communication channels throughout the course of the health threat.

International data transfers and storage location(s)

Your data will be shared between UK Salesforce Service Cloud and Germany Salesforce Marketing Cloud.

Retention and disposal policy

Data and call recordings will be stored for 3 months. After which they will be securely destroyed.

How we keep your data secure

We will only collect data that is relevant to monitoring and containment of public health matters, specific to the identified health threat.

We have designed the solution and our processes to ensure health data is not included in the content of the SMS itself. Where health data is required, alternative encrypted and secure channels will be used in the campaigns (e.g. via email) or additional solutions will be integrated such as links out to secure web based questionnaires.

Your rights as a data subject

By law, data subjects have a number of rights and this processing does not take away or reduce these rights under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (2016/679) and the UK Data Protection Act 2018 applies.

These rights are:

  1. The right to get copies of information – individuals have the right to ask for a copy of any information about them that is used.
  2. The right to get information corrected – individuals have the right to ask for any information held about them that they think is inaccurate, to be corrected.
  3. The right to limit how the information is used – individuals have the right to ask for any of the information held about them to be restricted, for example, if they think inaccurate information is being used.
  4. The right to object to the information being used – individuals can ask for any information held about them to not be used. However, this is not an absolute right, and continued use of the information may be necessary, with individuals being advised if this is the case.
  5. The right to get information deleted – this is not an absolute right, and continued use of the information may be necessary, with individuals being advised if this is the case.
